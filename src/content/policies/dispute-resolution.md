## Overview
This document outlines the mandatory legal steps that must be taken to resolve conflicts before a civil lawsuit can be filed by the Association or an Owner. 

## Internal Dispute Resolution (IDR)
The IDR process allows an Owner to request a "meet and confer" with a designated Board director to resolve the dispute informally. The Association cannot refuse this request, and the Member cannot be charged a fee to participate.

## Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)
If IDR fails, the ADR process mandates mediation, and if necessary, binding arbitration. By participating in arbitration, parties expressly waive their right to a jury trial.

## Small Claims Exception
While arbitration is the standard, a Member retains the right to bypass arbitration and seek relief in Small Claims Court for disputes that fall within that court's financial jurisdiction (typically claims under $10,000).
